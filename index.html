How this app works

Today

  • Shows the session plan for whatever week/day is selected. Tap a day pill or use Prev/Next week to browse — tap Jump to today to snap to the real calendar date (set your Week-1 Monday once; it remembers after that).
  • Thursdays automatically switch to League Day mode, pulling team/bib info from Teams.
  • Mon–Wed sessions follow a standard rhythm — Circle Up → Skills → SEL Game → Close Out — tagged right on each block below so you can see the shape of a complete skills session at a glance. A session is missing something if it has no Circle Up to open or close it; not every day has a distinct game block, and that's OK.
  • The one-line reflection box is optional, private to this device, and not scored or shared.

Bank

  • Every drill, game, warm-up, cool-down, breath exercise, and SEL prompt in the program — searchable and filterable by type, skill, value, and length.
  • Tap Coach details for equipment/space/source, and Step-by-step for a follow-along execution guide.
  • Tap + Supplies on any drill to add its equipment straight to your Materials request.
  • Tap + Add to session on any drills you want to bundle, then use the bar at the bottom of the screen to download a handout for another coach.
  • See full drill → — think of this button like a shortcut. On the Today page you only see the short version of a drill: just enough to run it. If you want the WHOLE thing — every step, what equipment you need, and the video — tap See full drill. It jumps you straight to that exact drill's full card over in the Bank, already opened up, so you don't have to search for it yourself.

Safety

  • Injury reports: fill in the form, tap Email to Mack & family — it opens your phone's mail app pre-filled, ready to send.
  • The SEL & Restorative Practices Guide and the adaptations library live here too — filter to the situation you're in for praise scripts, de-escalation steps, and repair conversations.

Teams

  • Thursdays: generate the weekly 3v3 league teams + bibs, then tap stats live during games.
  • Mon–Wed: quick balanced scrimmage teams, plus the stations/courts calculator for splitting a group across hoops.
  • Both use the Roster's league rating to balance teams.
  • Roster (names, allergies, IEP/504, emergency contacts) lives right here now. Add students one at a time or tap Import CSV to load a whole list at once.

Materials

  • Tap the Materials button (top right, any tab) to open your request cart, add common-kit or first-aid items or supplies for a specific drill, and email the list straight to Mack.

Skill snapshot

  • Week 1 Monday (baseline) and the Week 5 shooting-form day (retest) each show a 5-station scoring grid. Rotate small groups through the stations and score every kid 1–5 per station, using the simple scale shown on the card.
  • Scores auto-set each kid's League rating (still visible/editable in Roster) so Teams can balance groups off real data instead of a guess.
  • At the Week 5 retest, each kid's Day-1 baseline shows as a small "B:" badge next to the new score, with an arrow showing growth since Day 1.

Safety, First Aid & CPR

Review every Monday. Always call 911 first if unsure. This is reference — not a replacement for certified Red Cross / American Heart Association training.

When to call 911 immediately

  • Unresponsive, not breathing, or only gasping
  • Suspected head, neck, or spine injury — do not move the player
  • Seizure that doesn't stop on its own within 1 minute
  • Severe bleeding that won't stop after 5 min of pressure
  • Open fracture / bone visible, or limb at a wrong angle
  • Severe allergic reaction (face/lip swelling, breathing trouble)
  • Heat stroke (hot dry skin OR confused/vomiting)
  • Chest pain or trouble breathing that doesn't resolve in 2 min

Default rule: water break every 15 minutes. Two whistles = water. Always.

Hands-Only CPR · 911 first

If a player collapses and isn't responding

  1. Check. Tap their shoulders and shout. No response, no normal breathing? → CPR.
  2. Call. Yell to a staffer: "Call 911 — get the AED — now." Don't leave the player.
  3. Push hard, push fast. Heel of one hand on the center of the chest, other hand on top. 2 inches deep, 100–120 per minute (to the beat of "Stayin' Alive" or "Crazy in Love"). Let the chest fully recoil between pushes.
  4. Use the AED as soon as it arrives. Follow the voice prompts — pads on bare chest, away from sweat/water.
  5. Keep going until EMS arrives and takes over. Trade compressions every 2 min to stay fresh.

For drowning, drug overdose, or kids under 8: 30 compressions → 2 rescue breaths → repeat (if you're trained in rescue breaths; otherwise compressions only).

What to do, by scenario

Click a scenario to expand its protocol. All in 60 seconds or less.

Head coach · 5-min pre-session
  • Floor swept & dry — no sweat puddles, no balls left from morning
  • Water cooler full + cups stacked
  • First-aid kit stocked & visible — check expiration on instant cold packs & ointments
  • AED location confirmed & phone has signal
  • Headcount matches roster — flag missing
  • Heat index check — >90°F? Move indoor / shorten
  • Know each kid's allergies / asthma / medical alerts (Doc 05)
Incident protocol
  1. Stop the activity. Send other students to a safe spot with a staffer.
  2. Address the injury. RICE for sprains: Rest, Ice, Compress, Elevate.
  3. Call parent / guardian.
  4. Fill out the Injury Report ↓ the same day.
  5. Log it in Doc 10 — Compliance, Safety & Incident Log.
  6. Notify Mack (mcharles@go3point.org) by EOD.
